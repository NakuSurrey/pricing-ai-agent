# Landlord Policy — Home Shield Let

**Policy reference:** HS-LET-2026
**Cover type:** Buildings, optional contents, loss of rent
**Applies to:** Property let to tenants on an assured shorthold tenancy (AST)

---

## 1. What is covered

The policy covers loss or damage to the insured let property caused by:

- Fire, smoke, lightning, and explosion
- Storm, flood, and escape of water
- Theft and malicious damage by persons other than the tenant
- Impact from vehicles, aircraft, or falling trees
- Subsidence, heave, and landslip, with a £1,500 excess

Optional extensions available:

- Contents cover for landlord-owned furnishings, up to £20,000
- Loss of rent up to 24 months following an insured event
- Accidental damage extension for tenant-caused non-malicious damage
- Legal expenses cover for tenancy disputes

## 2. What is not covered

- Malicious damage by the tenant or any guest of the tenant — unless the
  optional malicious damage extension is purchased and the tenant has been
  referenced and deposit-protected
- Damage during periods the property is unlet for more than 60 consecutive
  days, unless agreed in writing and the property is inspected weekly
- Tenant's own possessions — these are the tenant's responsibility
- Rent arrears — this is a rent guarantee product, sold separately
- Cannabis cultivation damage or any damage arising from criminal use

## 3. Excess

- Standard claims: £500
- Subsidence: £1,500
- Escape of water: £500
- Malicious damage by tenant (if extension purchased): £1,000

## 4. Tenant referencing requirement

Cover is conditional on written references being obtained for every tenant
at the start of the tenancy, including:

- Previous landlord reference or credit check
- Employer or income reference
- Photographic ID

Failure to reference a tenant may invalidate malicious damage claims.

## 5. Unoccupancy

Properties unoccupied for more than 60 consecutive days must be notified.
Cover during unoccupancy is restricted to fire, lightning, explosion, and
aircraft damage only. Water must be turned off at the mains.

## 6. Premium factors

Base premium is adjusted for:

- Property type and number of bedrooms
- Tenant type — professional, family, student, benefits claimant
- Number of tenants
- Rental value
- Claims history on any let property in the last 5 years
- Whether a letting agent manages the property
