# Flood Zone Extension — Home Shield Flood Guard

**Policy reference:** HS-FLD-2026
**Cover type:** Flood-specific extension to the Standard Homeowner Policy
**Applies to:** Properties in designated high-risk or medium-risk flood zones
as defined by the Environment Agency flood map

---

## 1. Why this extension exists

The Standard Homeowner Policy provides flood cover only for properties in
standard (low) flood-risk zones. Properties in high or medium flood-risk
zones need this extension to receive flood cover. Without this extension,
flood claims on a high-risk property will be declined.

## 2. What is covered

- Damage to the building caused by flood from rivers, the sea, or surface
  water run-off
- Damage to contents caused by the same flood event
- Alternative accommodation for up to 12 months while the property is
  uninhabitable after a flood
- Professional drying and sanitation costs
- Replacement of flooring and plasterwork affected by flood water

## 3. What is not covered

- Gradual damp, rising damp, or damage from damp proof course failure
- Damage from leaking roofs or blocked gutters — this is a maintenance issue
- Properties below ground level (basements and cellars) unless declared and
  accepted at inception
- Damage to paths, patios, fences, or boundary walls
- Loss of value because the property sits in a flood zone

## 4. Flood Re scheme

Eligible properties are ceded to the Flood Re reinsurance scheme. Flood Re
caps the flood-specific portion of the premium based on council tax band.
Properties built after 1 January 2009 are not eligible for Flood Re.

## 5. Excess

A mandatory flood excess applies to each flood claim:

- Council tax bands A to C: £250
- Council tax bands D to F: £500
- Council tax bands G to H: £1,000
- Non-Flood Re properties (post-2009 build): £2,500

## 6. Flood resilience requirements

To keep the extension active, the following are required:

- Property assessment by an approved surveyor at inception
- Flood barriers installed at all ground-floor external doors
- Non-return valves on all drains below the first-floor level
- Electrical sockets mounted at least 1.5 metres above floor level, where
  practical

## 7. Premium factors

Premium reflects:

- Flood zone risk rating (high, medium, standard)
- Historic flood claims at the postcode
- Presence of flood resilience features (see section 6)
- Whether the property qualifies for Flood Re
- Ground floor elevation above mean sea level
